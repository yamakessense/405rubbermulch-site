Arkansas Tire Accountability Program (TAP)

The Arkansas Department of Environmental Quality (DEQ) runs the Tire Accountability Program (TAP) under Act 317, which is required to develop market opportunities for the beneficial use of used tire material. Unlike a direct school-purchase grant, Arkansas routes funding through market and economic-stimulus incentive grants to used-tire programs, local governments, or state agencies, and through regional used-tire management districts.

What to know

DEQ can use up to 10% of the Used Tire Recycling Fund each year for grants. Market and economic-stimulus incentive grants go to eligible used-tire programs, local government, or a state agency, board, or commission. Arkansas is required by Act 317 to develop markets for beneficial use of used tire material, including playground and surfacing uses. Schools and parks typically work through their regional used-tire management district rather than applying directly.

Why rubber mulch for playgrounds

IPEMA-certified rubber mulch provides consistent fall-height protection, doesn't compact or decompose like wood fiber, won't blow or float away as easily, and doesn't need yearly topping off. For schools, parks and daycares it means a safer surface and far less maintenance over its life — which is exactly why state tire programs encourage it as a recycled-tire end use.
