Oklahoma DEQ & recycled-tire funding

The Oklahoma Department of Environmental Quality (DEQ) runs the Used Tire Recycling Program and supports market development for recycled tire materials in uses like playgrounds and trails. DEQ's community funding is structured around its Funding Opportunities for Communities program, with grants generally available to units of government, tribal entities, components of the Oklahoma State System of Higher Education, and qualifying sole-source nonprofits.

What to know

DEQ encourages recycled-tire end uses such as playground surfacing and trails as part of its tire program mission. Community grants generally run from $1,000 to $50,000, with reimbursement structures that vary by grant track. Eligible applicants include units of government, tribal entities, higher-education components, and certain sole-source nonprofits. Funding is limited and not guaranteed, and depends on monies available in the Used Tire Recycling Indemnity Fund.

Why rubber mulch for playgrounds

IPEMA-certified rubber mulch provides consistent fall-height protection, doesn't compact or decompose like wood fiber, won't blow or float away as easily, and doesn't need yearly topping off. For schools, parks and daycares it means a safer surface and far less maintenance over its life — which is exactly why state tire programs encourage it as a recycled-tire end use.
